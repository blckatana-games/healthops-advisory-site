Our approach · A new engagement

What we can do for you.

We understand how your organization works today before recommending changes. Most operations projects fail when solutions arrive before the underlying workflow, staffing, and data are understood — so we work in deliberate phases.

The engagement, phase by phase

An eight-phase framework

1
Weeks 1–2

Discovery

Understand the current state. Meet key stakeholders and review the organization’s workflows, policies, staffing, financials, quality metrics, and technology.

Executive leadershipMedical directorsNursing leadershipFront desk & schedulingBilling / revenue cycleIT / EHR support
2
Observe

Observe Operations

Watch the work happen rather than relying only on meetings — from check-in and rooming to referrals, discharge, and billing. The biggest inefficiencies often become obvious on the floor.

BottlenecksDuplicate workManual processesWaiting timeCommunication gaps
3
Baseline

Gather Data

Build a baseline across patient access, clinical operations, revenue cycle, financial, and quality KPIs — the numbers every later decision is measured against.

Appointment wait timeNo-show rateDays in A/RClaim denial rateCost per visitPatient satisfaction
4
Analyze

Identify Root Causes

Avoid jumping to solutions. For every problem we ask why it happens — staffing, training, technology, or policy — using process mapping, the 5 Whys, and Pareto analysis.

Process mappingSwimlane diagramsRoot cause analysis5 WhysPareto analysis
5
Prioritize

Prioritize Opportunities

Not every issue should be addressed at once. We plot impact against effort to focus first on initiatives with measurable return.

High impact / low effort → immediateQuick improvementsStrategic projects
6
Plan

Develop an Improvement Plan

Each recommendation includes current state, problem statement, proposed solution, resources, timeline, risks, expected outcomes, and KPIs to measure success.

Current & future stateResources & timelineExpected outcomesSuccess KPIs
7
Pilot

Implement in Small Steps

Pilot one clinic or unit, measure results, adjust, then expand. Healthcare organizations respond better to incremental change than large-scale transformation.

PilotMeasureAdjustExpand gradually
8
Sustain

Measure Success

Create a dashboard leadership can review regularly — throughput, wait time, provider productivity, revenue, overtime, turnover, satisfaction, and quality.

Live KPI dashboardGovernance cadenceRegular reporting

Balancing risk & return

Improvements that hold up under scrutiny.

Every recommendation weighs operational and regulatory risk against expected return — so gains in efficiency never come at the expense of compliance, financial stability, or patient safety.

Guiding principles

Evaluated through four lenses

We frame every operational area around four pillars, and make recommendations that are practical, measurable, and sustainable — not simply new tasks or technology.

01

People

Involve frontline staff early — they know where inefficiencies live.

02

Processes

Let data drive decisions rather than assumptions.

03

Technology

Consider regulatory and compliance needs in every recommendation.

04

Performance

Tie every improvement to measurable outcomes and report often.
